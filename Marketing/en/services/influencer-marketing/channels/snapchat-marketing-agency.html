What is Snapchat?

Snapchat became famous in the Western World in 2012 and reinvented the way social media platforms worked. Prior to Snapchat, social media was very desktop-based, and it was all about accumulating data, posting tweets, statuses, videos being uploaded. Snapchat changed all of this.

"Snapchat isn’t about capturing the traditional Kodak moment. It’s about communicating with the full range of human emotion — not just what appears to be pretty or perfect."

Snapchat's initial core concept of the app is that any picture or video or message you send - by default - is made available to the receiver for only a short time before it becomes inaccessible. Private, person-to-person photo sharing really did change the game!

Snapchat: An Emerging Haven For Influencer Marketing

Since its launch in 2011, Snapchat has become a surprisingly formidable competitor in the social media space. Originally conceived as a messaging app, Snapchat is noted for its unique ephemeral approach, with content fading soon after it is viewed, allowing for natural-feeling conversations.

Therefore, to be an avid Snapchat user is to be committed to the platform – and this helps to explain why Snapchat-based influencers often achieve impressive engagement rates with their content. You, too, can benefit from such engagement when you partner with a Snapchat influencer.

Benefit From Engaging Content In Just A Snap

Snapchat’s core functionality enables users to make and send multimedia messages – “snaps” – decorated with filters, text captions and drawings. Hence, users are encouraged to unleash their creative streak, but this isn’t the only reason why their content can quickly attract interest.

As Snapchat was built as a messaging app first and a social media platform second, users who do opt to follow influencers are often strongly committed to doing so. Furthermore, as they know that the influencer’s content will quickly fade, they are incentivised to view it quickly.
