4 Brands That Are Killing It With Influencer Marketing

The social media space changes everyday, no really, every day. Brands constantly look for ways to get their messaging out and increase awareness about their products. In the last 5 years, there definitely has been an increase in brands leveraging influencers to help break through the clutter. Consumers are smart and prefer to avoid content that is heavily branded with traditional Marketing messaging so how do you reach them?

In my 5+ years in Influencer Marketing specifically, I have grown an affinity for a handful of brands that are doing it right, reaching their target audience and creating some amazing content. Here are 4 that continue to stay on my radar.

Taco Bell

Taco Bell does a great job of reaching their audience of millennial, high energy consumers and creating content that they can share. The added bonus is when they bring on great influencers like Snapchat influencer, Shonduras to create original content and share Taco Bell messaging (in his own way) on his social channels.


Madewell

The women’s clothing company knows how to reach their audience. An example of this was when they partnered with five influencers, including Bethany Marie, to increase awareness about the anniversary of their signature tote. Their campaign included great images from dedicated Instagram influencers, similar to the look and feel they share overall but with the anniversary tote and the hashtag, #TOTEWELL. With all influencer posts combined, the campaign reached over one million consumers on Instagram with on target, quality content.


Maybelline

I am a fan of influencers that show their favorite makeup and skincare products. I get the monthly ipsy and Birchbox packages with new makeup and skincare samples to try and love seeing when some of my favorite influencers share what makeup brands they enjoy. A recent post from influencer and friend, Melinda Fleming on Instagram of her Maybelline 24k eye shadow palette is fun and in line with other content she has posted. She shares a link to her blog with a resourceful 3 step make-up routine and ties in the copy with the dedicated #24knudes hashtag so consumers can search for others that are using the product.


Apple

Apple recently has started to partner with great YouTube influencers to help spread the word about products that probably don’t need promotion. Their rationale for working with trusted influencers takes them from a great tech brand to a cool, acquirable brand that everyone wants. One of my favorite influencers who shares her love for Apple frequently is a YouTuber that I have worked a lot with in the past, iJustine. She is a huge Apple fan and does a great jobs sharing Apple products, key messaging and the things that she thinks her fans will love. Here’s a great video that she shared of her “unboxing” the Apple Watch when it first came out. If you check out her YouTube and Instagram channels, you will see her passion for the brand.


While sharing the right content is only one piece of the influencer pie, knowing how to pitch them, who to pitch for their partnership (talent manager, MCN, lawyer, etc.), what their ROI should be and ensuring they are FTC compliant shows how complicated this type of Marketing has become for brands. It's definitely not rocket science but instead a specialized field that takes the right experience and relationships.
