T&C Wedding: Toast and Finn

The Instagram-famous pair tied the knot at New York's High Line Hotel this Wednesday.

By Diana Bruk
Jan 14, 2016
Sophie Elgort/Zola Registry

Instagram celebrity Toast and fellow Instagram celebrity Finn were married in the historic Refectory of New York's High Line Hotel on January 13, 2016.

Toast, 10, is a Cavalier King Charles Spaniel and the canine companion of Internet star The Fat Jew (nee Josh Ostrovsky) and his wife Katie Sturino. Finn, 7, is a Dachshund-Chihuahua mix and the canine companion of Amanda Hearst, the heiress to the Hearst Corporation and founder of Friends of Finn, a committee of The Humane Society of the United States which aims to stop the inhumane treatment of dogs at puppy mills. Since Toast and Finn are puppy mill survivors themselves, a portion of the proceeds from the gift registry and ticket sales ($150 a head) of the wedding will be donated to National Mill Dog Rescue.

The couple met in the summer, while vacationing in the Hamptons. They immediately connected over their shared background and their mutual commitment to ending the mistreatment of animals in illegal breeding facilities. Love soon blossomed, and in the fall Finn proposed on a beautiful day in Central Park.

"They have a classic New York love story," Sturino told me. "The day before the wedding, Toast went in for a final fitting, and then she and the bridesmaids had a Nancy Meyers movie marathon while drinking White Girl Rose."

The bride wore a custom gown by Marchesa which featured delicate tufts of tulle and an elegant beadwork pattern on the back. A sparkling headband and white veil crowned her auburn trestles of hair, and a $139,000 diamond necklace on loan from London Jewelers regally gleamed on her copper neck.

Over 300 guests gathered to watch Toast be led down the aisle by her godfather, fashion mogul Simon Doonan.

Sophie Elgort/Zola Registry

She was joined by her bridesmaids, Instagram-famous celebrities Chloe the Mini Frenchie,Ella Bean,Muppet,Pants, andCarole Radziwill's goldendoodle,Baby, who all wore matching pink cashmere sweaters.

The ceremony was officiated by New York Post gossip columnist Cindy Adams, who asked the dogs to promise to share the couch during reruns of Lassie and every last bit of cheese, and to protect one another from larger canine breeds.

"Speak now, or forever hold your pee," she said, as they shared their "I dos."

Sophie Elgort/Zola Registry

The guests then enjoyed a variety of light canapes and signature drinks sponsored by Smirnoff and White Girl Rose: a twist on the Moscow Mule called the "Toast'ed Winter Mule" in honor of the bride, and a classic dry martini called the "Chiweenie Tini" in honor of the groom. The canine guests were supplied with their own light bites in the form of glass pods. Naturally, they ate first.

The bride was not available for comment, but she seemed very pleased as she posed for photos and licked her bridesmaids with her signature protruding tongue, before laying down to rest, her head poised in a look of refined content.

Diana Bruk

After cutting the cake, supplied by Butterfly Bakeshop, she and Finn retired to their room for their first night together as a married couple. Some of the other canine guests were not as patient with their amorous intentions.

As for the honeymoon, the pair have not yet finalized their plans.

"We're thinking a long weekend together in the Hamptons," Sturino said.

Classic New York.
